CSS Typeface Process

Beginning

It started with research into typography that used shapes. I researched different oddly shapes. I was primarily inspired by my current mixed emotions of feeling disconnected yet still having a support system. I was previously living in the UK with my long-term partner, and I had this support system that a lot of people did not have because of the pandemic. Then I had to move back to Canada, and I was more disjointed from it than a complete disconnect, since I still have that connection with my partner. I wanted to primarily create letters that were disjointed, but still legible and still connected.

CSS Versions

Trying to convert my sketches into CSS was the most difficult part of this project. I started out with basic sans-serif font.

This was an experiment with using curves and the border-radius tag.

Back to my original goal of trying to keep sharp diagonal lines in my design.

I also experimented with introducing shadows to my type, but it ended up being more time consuming and abandoned the idea.

I struggled trying to disjoint the letters and keep the type consistent throughout. I ended up changing the position of triangle shapes to hopefully show a disjointed aesthetic.
